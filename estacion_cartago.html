Estación de Cartago

Cartago was the railway's first major terminus, reached when the Cartago–Alajuela segment opened in 1873 under the original Meiggs–Keith contract — seventeen years before the line was extended all the way to the Caribbean. The station building is a simple single-storey wooden structure with three rooms, built in the early twentieth century; the surviving terminal building and the adjoining right-of-way are what remain of what was once a much larger station yard, most of which has since been built over.

The approach to Cartago from the San José side descends through El Alto (Ochomogo) and crosses the Puente del Fierro (Iron Bridge) just before reaching the station — a crossing still referenced in modern photographs of the revived Interurbano tourist service, which uses twin-unit "Apolo" railcars on this same route today.

Sources: FECOSA station archive; "Nuevo servicio a Cartago" (FECOSA); Mi Costa Rica de Antaño.
